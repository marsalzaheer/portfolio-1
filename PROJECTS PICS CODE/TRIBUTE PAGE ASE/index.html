Here's a timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1928 - Abdul Sattar Edhi was born on February 28 in Bantva, Gujarat, British India. His family was part of the Muslim community that migrated to Pakistan after the partition.
  • 1947 - At the age of 19, Edhi migrated to Karachi, Pakistan, with his family following the partition of India. The move was part of the massive population shift that accompanied the creation of Pakistan.
  • 1951 - Edhi founded his first free dispensary in Karachi, focusing on providing medical care to the poor. His initial goal was to offer basic healthcare services to those who could not afford them.
  • 1957 - Edhi established the Edhi Foundation, initially setting up an ambulance service to provide free emergency medical services. This was the beginning of what would become the largest volunteer ambulance network in the world.
  • 1965 - The Edhi Foundation expanded its services to include orphanages, homeless shelters, and clinics. This period marked significant growth in the foundation's capacity to provide social welfare services.
  • 1974 - Edhi's vision extended further with the establishment of the Edhi Foundation's first maternity home. The foundation also began offering comprehensive emergency medical services, including an extensive network of ambulances.
  • 1980s - During this decade, Edhi's humanitarian efforts gained international recognition. His work was highlighted for its extensive reach and impact, including the establishment of hospitals, educational institutions, and other welfare services.
  • 1990s - The Edhi Foundation continued to grow, expanding its services to include educational institutions, hospitals, and a comprehensive social welfare system. The foundation also developed a robust network of facilities for the needy across Pakistan.
  • 2005 - Edhi was honored with the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Public Service, a prestigious recognition of his humanitarian work and dedication to serving the underprivileged.
  • 2010 - Edhi's health began to decline due to kidney failure. Despite his worsening condition, he continued to work and oversee the operations of the Edhi Foundation, demonstrating his unwavering commitment to his cause.
  • 2013 - Edhi refused to seek treatment abroad for his kidney condition, choosing instead to receive care locally. His decision highlighted his dedication to staying connected with his work and his people.
  • 2016 - Abdul Sattar Edhi passed away on July 8 in Karachi, Pakistan, at the age of 88. His death marked the end of an era for the Edhi Foundation, but his legacy of humanitarian work continued through the ongoing operations of the foundation.
